Cristopher Columbus

Cristopher Columbus was a self-educated Italian explorer who found a sponsor in Queen Isabella and King Ferdinand of Spain. With three ships, he traveled for several months across the Atlantic Ocean finally landing in the Bahamas. He returned to the Caribbean three times after his first expedition, but his strained relationship with the Spanish crown ultimately led to his arrest and removal from his position of power in the new Spanish colonies.

Francisco Pizarro

Born into a poor family, Pizarro was a Spanish conquistador who pursued fame and fortune in the Americas. He was one of the first Europeans to reach the Pacific Ocean, serving as an official of Spain's new colony in Panama. Later, he led an expedition to Peru that resulted in the fall of Inca Empire and Spanish colonization of Peru.

Hernan Cortes

Like other men of lesser nobility, Hernan Cortes sought riches and adventure in the Americas. He is famous for leading an expedition to the interior of present-day Mexico. He is often credited with bringing down the Aztec Empire and bringing much of mainland Mexico into the control of the Spanish.

Juan Ponce de Leon

A Spanish noble, Ponce de Leon was an explorer for Spain and later the governor of Puerto Rico. In search of the fountain of youth, he explored the lands around Puerto Rico, and in 1513, led an expedition to the coast of Florida.
